Why it matters

ClickFix tests instruction compliance, not just link clicks

A lot of phishing simulations stop at the click. ClickFix measures something different: whether a target will trust a page enough to follow a fake technical workflow that feels routine, urgent, or legitimate. That makes it a strong fit for teams trying to test the newest wave of social engineering instead of only recycling old credential-harvest templates.

What the Framework adds

  • Point-and-click ClickFix landing page template
  • Harmless callback verification instead of payload delivery
  • Live console visibility when the event happens
  • Reporting metric for ClickFix callbacks
  • Training mapped to the exact user behavior

Built-in template

Operators do not need to hand-build a fake verification page or bolt on separate analytics. ClickFix is available directly inside the landing page workflow, alongside the rest of the platform's phishing templates.

Measured outcome

The landing page records a harmless callback when the target follows the prompt, giving you a stronger signal than a page visit alone.

Actionable remediation

Instead of generic awareness content, the Framework can tie training to the exact technique the user fell for in that exact campaign. Training is included and optional, not sold as a separate add-on.

What makes the result more useful

A realistic technique is only valuable if it connects to the rest of the engagement. PhishU Framework keeps ClickFix in the same workflow as domains, landing pages, email delivery, analytics, and targeted remediation. That makes the outcome easier to explain to clients, security teams, and executives.

Purpose-built for safe, authorized testing

PhishU is designed to help defenders measure risky user behavior, not to package unsafe outcomes. In the ClickFix workflow, the verification request calls back to the landing page and records the event as analytics. That gives internal teams, pentest firms, MSSPs, and red teams a realistic behavioral test without turning the landing page into something it should not be.

It also fits the broader PhishU philosophy: get caught, then join the school. When training is used, the recipient can be shown exactly what happened, exactly what they missed, and exactly what to watch for next time.
