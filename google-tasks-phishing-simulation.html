Why Google Tasks phishing matters

The public Google Tasks phishing technique is important because the message does not ask users to judge a strange sender domain. It appears to come through a trusted Google workflow. That changes how mail security tools, users, and help desk teams interpret the message.

A realistic assessment needs to test that exact failure mode. Can users spot a suspicious workflow when the sender looks legitimate? Can defenders detect the campaign when the delivery path is not a typical phishing mailbox? Can training explain why trusted cloud notifications still need scrutiny?
